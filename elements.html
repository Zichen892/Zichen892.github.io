The objective of this work is to provide a database management system for a new application called Treat Your Pet. This new idea seeks to provide customized affordable services to pet owners all in one platform where they can choose their provider based on the fit and ratings from other customers. The database designed and implemented, allocates all the service providers and pet owners' information for an efficient matching for both parties in this new prolific market in the United States. Without an integral system for this industry, people were having difficulties finding special pet services such as urgent pet care, transportation, and so on. Also, service providers do not have access to the entire market due to the information gap. Therefore, a database management system was needed to connect service providers and customers. This database management system will not only bridge the mentioned gap but also benefits service providers with data analysis by retrieving and analyzing information to improve their business performance.

The database was modeled relying on data provided by pet owners and service providers. The main implementation consists of a relational database following the right protocols for an efficient system. The design of it starts with EER and UML conceptual models. Then we mapped the conceptual model to the relational data model, which focuses on reducing the system redundancy and better managing the database in the future by normalizing the structure. Finally, we implemented in MySQL a relational database with the product of previous steps. Additionally to this, we created a proof-of-concept of specific parts of the data model leveraging NoSQL database systems with a document-based MongoDB system and graph-based Neo4j database.

This database system has three main achievements based on system performance, business analysis, and social networking. The system provided an efficient way to allocate all sources in the pet service industry, which reduced the gap between demand and supply. Also, by the connection with open-source language Python, data analysis can be provided in a timely manner to service providers in order to evaluate their performance on price, service hours, rating, etc. Lastly, with the implementation in Neo4j, we are able to implement a social network aspect to the application that will provide a sense of community to customers with an efficient way of processing and querying information.

  

 

 

 

I. Introduction

New generations are deciding not to have children and instead, adopted a tendency to get pets. This has been a boom in the pet supplies industry as there is a whole new market for more human-like services for pets (eg daycare, sitting, hotels, etc). In 2020, pet industry sales exceeded $100 billion and it is expected to triple in the near future.[1] Given the pandemic and people’s busy agenda, having the opportunity to order services for dogs online is becoming critically important for this market. Over the lockdowns, pet owners shopping online increased by almost 20% and this is a trend that will continue. That is the reason why providing a centralized app that connects pet services and supplies with pet owners is so important. Our project is the start of this by designing a database for the application that would be offered in the US market. The main goal of the APP would be to provide customized affordable services to pet owners all in one platform where they can choose their provider based on the fit and ratings from other customers.

Requirements:

To use the app, a pet owner needs to create an account and provide information about themself (full name, birth date and email account, phone number and address). A pet owner can have several pets associated with their account and requires to report at least one. From the pet, it is required to store name, type of pet, breed, birth date, brand of  food, any medical issues (can be multiple), among others. It is also required for a service provider to create a business account. This type of account is available for individuals and businesses. For both types of business accounts, we want to capture a short introduction/biography. For persons (individuals) such as dog walkers, it is required to provide full name, phone number, e-mail, among others. For businesses, it is required to store business certifications, addresses (can be multiple in this case), phone number(s), e-mail.. A service provider can provide multiple service types. When a service is hired through the app, it is important to store information regarding service type, time and date of it, as well as the price agreed. Also, when a service is provided, we would like to obtain a client’s rating of the service to show later in the app. There is also the functionality of customer earning reward points every time that a service is hired.

III. Mapping Conceptual Model to Relational Model

●               PetOwner(UserID, Birthdate, OwnerEmail, OwnerPhoneNumber,  RewardPoints, OwnerAddress, OwnerFirstName, OwnerLastName)

●               Service(TypeID, TypeDescription)

●               ServiceProvider(ProviderID, Bio )

○               ProviderID is a foreign key referring to ProviderID on ServiceProvider, declared as NOT NULL

●               BusinessAddress(ProviderID, BusinessAddress)

○               ProviderID is a foregin key referring to ProviderID on Business table, declared as NOT NULL

●               BusinessPhoneNumber(ProviderID, BusinessPhoneNumber)

○               ProviderID is a foreign key referring to ProviderID on ServiceProvider table, declared as NOT NULL

●               Hires(ServiceID, ProviderID, PetName, UserID, TypeID, Price, Rating, DateTime)

○               ProviderID, UserID, PetName and TypeID are foreign keys referring to original variables with same names in tables ServiceProvider, PetOwner, Pet, and Service accordingly, all declared as NOT NULL

Note: Underlined variables are primary keys and the ones in italics are foreign keys

IV. Implementation of Relation Model via MySQL and NoSQL

Implementation of the relational model was done locally using MySQL. The following are some queries that provide relevant information regarding the database:

Query 1:  Who has hired more than once a service and at least one of those was dog meal delivery that lives in California?  

SELECT H.USERID, AGG.OWNERFIRSTNAME, AGG.OWNERLASTNAME, COUNT(*) AS NUMBER_SERVICES_HIRED

FROM HIRES AS H

INNER JOIN (SELECT USERID, OWNERFIRSTNAME, OWNERLASTNAME, SUBSTRING_INDEX(SUBSTRING_INDEX(OWNERADDRESS, ',', -2),',',1) AS STATE FROM PETOWNER ) AS AGG ON H.USERID = AGG.USERID

INNER JOIN SERVICE AS S ON S.TYPEID = H.TYPEID

WHERE AGG.STATE = "CA" AND S.TYPEDESCRIPTION = "Dog meal delivery"

GROUP BY H.USERID;

 

 

Query 2: Who own pets with medical issues“Anxiety” and “Obesity”? (Show pets name, owner’s name and all their medical issues)

SELECT PO.OWNERFIRSTNAME, PO.OWNERLASTNAME, P.PETNAME, M.MEDISSUE FROM PETOWNER AS PO, PET AS P, MEDISSUE AS M

WHERE PO.USERID = P.USERID AND P.PETNAME = M.PETNAME

AND P.USERID = M.USERID AND EXISTS (SELECT * FROM MEDISSUE M2

WHERE MEDISSUE = "Anxiety" AND M.PETNAME = M2.PETNAME AND PO.USERID = M2.USERID) AND EXISTS (SELECT * FROM MEDISSUE M2 WHERE MEDISSUE = "Obesity" AND M.PETNAME = M2.PETNAME AND PO.USERID = M2.USERID);

 

Query 3: What type of services have not yet  been requested?

SELECT TYPEDESCRIPTION FROM SERVICE AS S

WHERE NOT EXISTS (SELECT * FROM HIRES AS H WHERE S.TYPEID = H.TYPEID);

Query 4: What is the number of services hired and average spend of dog owners by number of pets owned? Sort by number of services and avg price descending

SELECT P.USERID, AGG.PETS_OWNED, COUNT(*) AS NUMBER_SERVICES_HIRED, AVG(PRICE) AS AVG_PRICE

FROM HIRES AS H INNER JOIN PET AS P ON H.PETNAME = P.PETNAME AND H.USERID = P.USERID LEFT JOIN (SELECT USERID, COUNT(*) AS PETS_OWNED FROM PET GROUP BY USERID) AS AGG ON P.USERID = AGG.USERID WHERE P.PETTYPE = "Dog"

GROUP BY P.USERID

ORDER BY NUMBER_SERVICES_HIRED DESC, AVG_PRICE DESC;

Query 5: What are the businesses providing services to the highest reward point holder? Only business

SELECT DISTINCT B.PROVIDERNAME FROM HIRES AS H, BUSINESS AS B

WHERE H.PROVIDERID = B.PROVIDERID AND H.USERID IN (SELECT USERID

    FROM PETOWNER AS PO WHERE REWARDPOINTS >= ALL ( SELECT        REWARDPOINTS FROM PETOWNER  ));

Query 6: What are the 3 highest value service transactions and their characteristics? (Show Pet owners with their pets’ name, pet type sorted by their price/cost)

SELECT H.USERID, H.PETNAME, P.PETTYPE, H.PRICE

 FROM HIRES AS H INNER JOIN PET AS P ON H.USERID = P.USERID

 WHERE H.PETNAME = P.PETNAME

 ORDER BY H.PRICE DESC;

                                                

Query 7: Which is the most popular hour to hire services in our app?

SELECT HOUR(DATETIME) AS HOURS, COUNT(*) AS FREQUENCY

FROM HIRES GROUP BY HOURS ORDER BY FREQUENCY DESC;

Query 8: Which business service provider had offered the lower price for each type of service?

SELECT B.PROVIDERNAME, S.TYPEDESCRIPTION,  H.PRICE

FROM HIRES AS H, BUSINESS AS B, SERVICE AS S

WHERE H.PROVIDERID = B.PROVIDERID AND H.TYPEID = S.TYPEID AND  H.PRICE <= ALL( SELECT H2.PRICE FROM HIRES AS H2, BUSINESS AS B2

WHERE H2.TYPEID = H.TYPEID AND H2.PROVIDERID = B2.PROVIDERID);

Query 9: Which business service providers have at least three business locations?

SELECT P.PROVIDERNAME FROM BUSINESSADDRESS B

INNER JOIN BUSINESS P  ON B.PROVIDERID = P.PROVIDERID

GROUP BY B.PROVIDERID  HAVING COUNT(*) >= 3

Query 10: How many services are being provided per month?

SELECT MONTH(DATETIME) AS MONTHS, COUNT(*) AS NUMBER_SERVICES

FROM HIRES GROUP BY MONTHS ORDER BY NUMBER_SERVICES DESC;

Query 11: Who are the providers with an average rating of at least 4 and provided a service that is more than $300?

WITH MAIN AS (SELECT H1.PROVIDERID, AVG(H1.RATING) AS AVG_RATING

FROM HIRES H1 GROUP BY H1.PROVIDERID HAVING AVG_RATING >=4)

SELECT M.PROVIDERID, M.AVG_RATING, H2.PRICE

FROM MAIN M LEFT JOIN HIRES H2 ON H2.PROVIDERID = M.PROVIDERID

HAVING H2.PRICE >=300;

MongoDB

We implemented in MongoDB Cloud 4 of the tables as collections with 10-12 documents each. Tables implemented were: Pet, Petowner, Hires, ServiceProvider. The connection and querying was done in python through library pymongo. Here are 3 of the queries ran:

Query 1: What is the total number of services per type of pet? Sorted descending

db.hires.aggregate([{"$lookup": {"from":"pet","localField":"petname","foreignField": "petname", "as": "petinfo"}},

{"$group":{"_id":"$petinfo.pettype", "count":{"$sum":1}}},

   {"$sort":{"count":-1}}])

Query 2: Which pets hired services? sort by the price from higher to lower

db.hires.aggregate([

   {"$group":{"_id":"$petname", "avg_price":{"$avg":"$price"}}},

   {"$sort":{"avg_price":-1}}])

Query 3:Which are the top 3 service providers  based on their avg rating?

db.hires.aggregate([{"$group":{"_id":"$providerid", "avg_rating":{"$avg":"$rating"}}},

   {"$sort":{"avg_rating":-1}},

   {"$limit":3}])

Note: MapReduce was not used as recent versions of MongoDB deprecated it and therefore it was not able to be implemented on this environment.

Neo4j

We implemented on the neo4j console a simple social network with 10 pet owners, where each can “follow” other pet owners. Here is a query performed:

How many people are each pet owner following that at least follows one person?  

MATCH (me:Petowner)-->(friend:Petowner) RETURN me.name, count(*) as number_friends ORDER BY number_friends DESC;

VII. Summary and recommendation

Throughout this project, we developed an implementation-ready RDBMS for a pet services app in MySQL with a proof of concept on MongoDB cloud and Neo4j at its console. The motivation behind this work is to offer a centralized app for the emerging and big market available for pet supplies and services in the US. This app will not only benefit the pet owners, but will also provide better ways of finding employment to several people that like animals and would like to pet sit, dog walk or provide other types of clever new services for them. Another advantage of this is that it is designed in a data driven way where informed decisions can be taken and provide relevant statistics to the businesses. The smooth and easy way of connecting the database with python makes this possible. We can create within seconds, plots that translate important performance messages from data. Additionally, with NoSQL database implementations, we can leverage its flexibility of content saved (e.g. in the future we might want to save pictures and be able to analyze them)  and easy manipulation of potential social network strategies in the app. 

Some of the improvements on this implementation are related to DevOps aspects of databases, basically on how to make sure everything lives in an easy to improve environment and that efficiently interacts with all the parts it has. First, for a full MongoDB implementation, it still needs to define the best way of grouping collections, as right now it is just a mirror of the normalized version of the relational model and this is likely not the most efficient way. The advantage is that this was set already on cloud, which will facilitate the future developments. Second, Neo4j was used in a simple environment where there is only interaction between pet owners. However, we can expand and have pets and service providers interacting, even a service can be provided as a partnership from different providers and for a group of dogs to utilize the many to many advantage of graph based databases.

In an era where everything has the potential to become Big Data, at some point we may need to consider using Spark and even Databricks services in conjunction with cloud storage to scale our current capacity and product. Finally, having a clear data governance plan and emphasize data quality is another aspect where future iterations should focus on, things like implementing constraints and other features from MySQL can help make sure that integrity and cardinalities of the design are correctly followed, for this some helpful features can be adding triggers and stored procedures.
